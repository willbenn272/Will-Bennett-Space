Origins of the World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee is a British computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. While working at CERN in 1989, he proposed a revolutionary system to link and access information over the internet using hypertext, which led to the development of the first website, web browser, and foundational technologies like HTML, HTTP, and URLs. His work transformed the internet from a research tool into the global web we use today. Berners-Lee continues to advocate for a free and open web through initiatives like the World Wide Web Foundation.
